Neat

Sunday 16 March 2025

Listen to me! While you read.

For a girl of my age, seventeen, suburban, I had, to my mind, solved the problem of boredom rather well. On a Friday night we passed you around and I pretended not to be repulsed by everyone’s saliva. I took you neat. You burned differently in the cold.

I thought I could dilute you with some weaker version of yourself. I misunderstood dilution. I accepted my punishment, but then, when the worst of it had passed, was overtaken with such dejection, such betrayal, that I sat slumped at the toilet unable to move.

A mistake, a huge mistake. In that moment, I had no doubt, I, Leesa, was a bad, bad girl. Please, God, I begged. I will be good. I will sleep at 9 o’clock every night, starting tomorrow. Make it stop!

But I will never forgive you for this one. I drank a glass of you, and then someone picked up a glass of me. They swirled it, held it up to the light. And there it was, all of it, even the most insoluble. They didn’t even have to try.

I was already neat.
