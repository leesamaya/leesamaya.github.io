About Love And Other Stories - Anton Chekhov

On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous - Ocean Voung. i love how his stories are set where there is NO hope. the american dream is a fallacy, so now what? this book shows u that life goes on and that sometimes u ahve to be gay in the corn fields instead of some big city. there is no escape fantasy of 'making it'. and yet the ppl in this book still shine, they still love, they still, in their hopeless reality, are kind to others knowing there's nothing in it for them

Women Who Run With The Wolves - Clarissa Pincourtella Esdes.

Memories, Dreams, Reflections - Carl Jung. i really like how when he broke from freud and hit rock bottom he had nothing to do except play with rocks and build model houses like he did as a kid. and it turned out to be the beginning his best work. maybe rockbottoms are a good place to be in to move straight toward your truest life direction. his (outside world) flop was so bad it meant he didn't see any other meaning metric like prestigue or social status as available and so all he could do was play and shit and sort of slide back into a primal default mode with only his natural impulses that eventually lead to his lifeswork. which makes me think how many mfs may have been geniuses is they didn't numb that demoralised, ego-crushed child state with drinking or whatever rockbottom ppl do when theyre at rock bottom. the man just sat in his boredom and unaddressed mysterious inner darkness and played. which led to his psychotic break but also the work he's most known for so its chilll like he lived in a cottage after that so now im thinking (hopefully i dont need to hit rock bottom for this) i should listen to my impulses more...? which is easier accesed when bored, or in nature. perhaps the next time it looks like i'm regressing in the outside world im really just cooking and moving toward my #truth

Youth - J.M. Coetzee. my god he is such a loser. no wonder he doesnt do interviews id be mortified writing a semi-autobiagraphical book exposing my lameness with such ruthless precision. like yes its semi but just how could someone have such intimate knowledge of loserdom without ever having been in it. whatever elusive persona he has irl this book ruins it

Man's Search For Meaning - Viktor Frankl. idk how to feel. i like it for what it's truth alone but i also think its a last resort cope. when so many ppl are suffering on mass bc of a broken system, then convincing individuals to see their fulfillment + sense of meaning in their lives are 100% their responsibility feels almost cruel. like holocaust jews were transcending mentally bc they were... prisoners about to get gassed?? this book selling millions of copies by ppl stuck inside a working-poor rut is a sign of social failure is it not
