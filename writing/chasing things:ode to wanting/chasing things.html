Chasing Things

An ode to wanting

We are always wanting, aren’t we? Want drags us from sleep, it makes us walk faster and think sharper. I tell myself I am capable, that everything I dream of is within reach. After all, I am Young. Hungry. A Conqueror. There's so much to grab, so much to chase. It makes my head spin.

Once, I told a boy I was hungry. Not for food. He worked at a startup I wanted to be at. He flicked a pencil with his thumb and index finger, the tip hitting my elbow - tap, tap, tap. Not accidental. Or maybe not? I couldn’t tell. He talked polite, his tone easy. His eyes: something else.

A secret language, I decided. Or maybe an equation:

1 tap = are you there?

2 taps = can you see me?

But I didn’t know the rest of the code.

We stood side by side, not looking at each other. I could only see our words, so ordinary, just like everyone else's. Had I imagined it?

But he was no longer just a boy anymore. He was something else. An alternate version of myself, maybe, from a life where I knew how to speak and where to stand, what to do with my hands. I met his gaze like a dare and tried on the version of me he seemed to see. Huh. Not bad.

Then, the question: Which one was real? The self he saw, or the one I saw? Or, let me say it another way: was my fate doomed, or not doomed? One world let me want.

But now I get it. (I say this with hesitation.) The in-between is enough. The quiet relief of it: permission to exist in the not yet.

Because, truthfully, I wasn’t who I said I was. No conqueror. I drink my coffee iced and sweet. I like movies where nothing much happens. I waste hours staring at trees like they’ve just appeared, their green overwhelming. I’m impatient, indecisive, and I never wait for the kettle to boil. Is that laziness? Or is it want, panic-fueled and burning inward, unable to decide if there’s a yet after the not? Maybe it’s just the excuse I tell myself.

I had left him that night, the boy, the tapping, the look in his eyes. I walked away, but the wanting stayed. It always does.
