We are always wanting, aren’t we? Want drags us from sleep, it makes us walk faster and think sharper. I tell myself I am a conqueror, that the world is mine to take, and the words swell in my chest like helium. I am Young. Hungry. I've got so much going for me, like come on, it drives me crazy.

Once, I told a boy I was hungry. Not for food. He worked at some startup I wanted to be at. He flicked a pencil with his thumb and index finger. The tip hit my elbow - tap, tap, tap. Not accidental. Or maybe not? I couldn’t tell. He talked polite, tone easy. His eyes: something else.

A secret language, I decided. Or maybe an equation:

1 tap = hello, are you there?

2 taps = can you see me?

But I didn’t know the rest of the code.

We stood side by side, not looking at each other. I could only see our words, so ordinary. Had I imagined it?

He was no longer a boy anymore. He was something else. An alternate version of myself, maybe, from a life where I knew what to say and how to stand, where to put my hands. I met his gaze like a dare and tried on the version of me he seemed to see. Huh. Not bad.

Then, the question: Which one was real? The me he saw, or the me I saw? Or, let me translate: was my fate doomed, or not doomed? One world let me want.

But now I get it. (I say this reluctantly.) The in-between is enough. The relief of it: to exist in the not yet.

Because, truthfully, I wasn’t who I said I was. No conqueror. I drink my coffee iced and sweet. I like movies where nothing much happens. I waste hours staring at trees like they’ve just appeared, their green overwhelming. I’m impatient, indecisive, and I never wait for the kettle to boil. Is that laziness? Or is it want burnt inwards, when the yet fades in and out after the not? Maybe it’s just the excuse I tell myself.

I had left that night. The boy, the tapping, the look in his eyes. I walked away, but the wanting stayed. It always does.
