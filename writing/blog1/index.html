I wind the car windows down, always. It is a small act, insignificant, though it feels like an act of defiance. The wind tangles my hair, but I don't care. Ah, sweet, sweet Summer! May this euphoria never wear off, the novelty of my newfound freedom to drive myself wherever I please. Just think of all the adventures ahead of me!

Today, strawberries: $3 for two. A steal. The gym, a return to my body. The library, long-overdue fashion magazines I only read a couple of pages of. All the little tasks that, together, make a life. In the car, Magnetising plays: a song like velvet stretched thin over wire. I am someone else now: a French spy with a cigarette in her mouth, driving a stolen car. I am invincible.
